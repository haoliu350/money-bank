Cyber Crime

Types of Cybercrime Acts and Preventive Measures

Some of the types of cybercrime that affect the ecommerce:

Phishing - This type of threat is when a scammer impersonates a trusted company and sends fake emails on behalf of the recipients so they are trust and provide the data requested by the alleged company.
Malware - It is a software that objectives to infiltrate or damage a computer without the owner's consent.
Pharming - It is a dangerous technique because the victim believes is visiting a legitimate website.
Crimeware - Crimeware is often associated with rootkits.
Clickjacking - is a vulnerability that affects browsers and other web products.
Account takeover - The scam occurs when the thieves obtain data from a user or a customer and take control of the account by changing the postal address or phone number to be able to perform online fraud.

Legal risk of the E-commerce

Never Over-prepared.

The priority rights - Information is more accessible and spread faster via e-commerce. Legal issues is not only associated with the corporate, actually it is from the household, enterprise and the society level. Before we start our own business online, there are multiple legal factors needed to be taken care: Avoiding any violation of the third-party trademarks right and protecting your own works by using the marks ™, ®, and/or © symbol, etc.

Then the business related law - As the traditional business, Such as the taxation, don’t hesitate to use online Terms of Use, etc.

Technology of the E-commerce

Opportunity as well as Threat.

Competition with the retail stores - The retail stores have the advantages on the user experience, the entry of the barrier of the e-commerce is low. The competition with the existing companies and substitutes are relative high. The new driver of the e-commerce have to be how to improve the user experience and the logistic management, etc.

Database management - The transaction data are huge, from the perceptive of the IT manager, we have to protect the security and consistency of the customer data and utilize data to make the business decision in an effective manner.

Mobile application - Recent researches show that the mobile applications is a blue ocean for E-commerce. That bring higher requirement to the information security and user experience.

Fraud in E-commerce

Never Overestimate the safety of E-commerce.

E-commerce transactions are much more likely to result in fraud than face-to-face ones. The reason is that, when in doubt about the legitimacy of a particular transaction, web-based merchants cannot physically verify the validity of the card or verify that the cardholder is an authorized user by requesting his driver’s license.

Data Breach

Online Crime.

Criminals are always looking for ways to circumvent the e-commerce merchants’ data protection mechanisms and steal the stored cardholder information. Sensitive data can be stolen online or at a physical location.
